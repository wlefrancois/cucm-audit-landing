How CCC works

The goal of CCC is simple: help you understand what is improving your readiness and what is holding you back. Instead of just collecting information, CCC is designed to help you make better daily decisions.

1

Log your day

Track core daily signals such as sleep, energy, inflammation, digestion, hydration, and mood.

2

Get your readiness score

CCC converts your daily inputs into a metabolic readiness score so you can quickly see where your system stands.

3

Improve what matters most

See limiting factors, recovery priorities, forecasts, and training guidance based on your data.

What to log

Sleep

How many hours you slept.

Example: 7.5 hours = solid recovery range for many people.

Energy

Rate your daily energy from 1 to 10.

1–3 = drained, 4–6 = average, 7–8 = strong, 9–10 = peak.

Inflammation

Rate how inflamed or irritated your body feels from 1 to 10.

1 = minimal, 5 = moderate, 10 = severe.

Digestion

Rate how stable and comfortable digestion felt from 1 to 10.

Higher is better. A lower score can signal food or stress issues.

Hydration

Log approximate water intake for the day.

Example: 80–100 oz often supports better readiness for many users.

Mood

Rate how steady, focused, or positive you felt from 1 to 10.

Lower mood can reflect stress, poor recovery, or unstable routines.

What you’ll see after logging

Metabolic Readiness

A simple score that helps you understand how ready your system is today.

Today’s Recommendation

Guidance based on what is currently limiting your score the most.

Recovery Priorities

The top areas to improve if you want a better result tomorrow.

Training Guidance

A simple view of whether today looks better for hard training, moderate work, or recovery.

What makes CCC different

Most health tools collect data. CCC is designed to help you use that data. The goal is not more noise — it is better visibility into what improves readiness, recovery, and consistency.

Ready to begin?

Your first log is the starting point. Once you enter a day, CCC can begin calculating your readiness, identifying limiting factors, and building your trend history.
